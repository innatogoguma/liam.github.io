My Usual Hobbies and Favorite Artist

Playing Keyboard

I started playing the keyboard when I was in 5th grade. I’m not bragging, but I could learn it faster than other kids back then, so I started studying music pieces through synthesia. Moreover, I am not into pop music or contemporary music; classical music has my heart. So, for 8 consecutive years, I have been playing musical pieces of Mozart, Beethoven, Liszt, Bach, etc. Playing the keyboard is a way of expressing myself musically, not just a pastime. It enables me to convert my feelings into sound, whether I'm learning a new piece, improvising melodies, or just playing to relax.

Cooking

Pardon me for the picture, I used my output in T.L.E because I don’t have any recent pictures of the dishes I made. Nevertheless, I really love cooking. It is something I picked up from my parents, who could turn the simplest ingredients into the most comforting meals. Their love for cooking inspired me to experiment in the kitchen, finding joy in creating dishes that bring warmth to those around me.

Solving Math Problems

Math may be the most hated subject for everyone, but for me, it is the most interesting. I genuinely enjoy dealing with mathematical problems since it tests my logic and enhances my critical thinking skills. Whether it's solving difficult problems or figuring out the most effective methods, I adore the structure and logic that go into mathematical ideas, especially in Calculus. I love solving derivatives and integrals, and I even came up with my own techniques for solving.

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

Mozart is my number one music artist. I like how his songs strike a balance between emotion and accuracy. I'm always fascinated by his music, whether it's the depth of his operas, the beauty of his piano pieces, or the genius of his symphonies. A trip into one of the greatest composers in history is what listening to Mozart is about.
